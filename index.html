About Me

I'm Emanuel Popa, a UX/UI designer

I moved to Stockholm, Sweden in 2015. At that time, I was working as a chef. Many of my friends were in the IT sector, which piqued my curiosity and led me to consider a career change. However, I wasn't sure which direction to take. I had a strong interest in design, so I began studying full-stack web development, thinking it would provide more opportunities. As I progressed with my studies, I realized that I wanted more control over the design aspect, and I found that programming didn't interest me as much as I initially thought. After obtaining my full-stack diploma, I decided to study UX design with the goal of gaining a better understanding of the creative process from idea to design. In the meantime, I started working as an assistant teacher at the same school for HTML & CSS and JavaScript courses, where I helped students grasp challenging concepts and corrected their projects for six months.

My motto is to balance design with usability, ensuring that neither aspect is neglected. While I studied UX two of my schoolmates and I founded a student organization of which I am one of the presidents. My role involves overseeing the courses. I frequently lead lessons and workshops for first-year students, covering topics such as HTML & CSS, as well as UX & UI design, which I thoroughly enjoy. I'm someone who enjoys having a lot on my plate.

What I do?

IX Design

I like to think not only about how the users interact with the content and the functionality, but also about how they interact with the device.

UX Research

The research is very important, especially in the early stage of a product. For me, research is about identifying the problem, analyzing it, and ultimately finding the solution.

UI Design

Forms, shapes, and colors are those elements that bring a visual product together; the ability lies in combining aesthetics with usability

Requirement Analysis

Gathering requirements for the project is very important. Here, combining all stakeholders' opinions and demands is crucial for the project.

Scrum Master

The project needs to move on quickly, and it needs to be ensured that the team is focused on priorities and the MVP. This is where the Scrum Master's role, who has the team's focus in mind, comes into play.

SEO Marketing

The product and the webpage need to have a good SEO score. Choosing the right keywords can be crucial, especially when you are new to the market.
